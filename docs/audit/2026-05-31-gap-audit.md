# Auditoria de Gaps — football-manager

**Data:** 2026-05-31 · **Método:** workflow multi-agente (10 auditores em paralelo por dimensão → verificação adversarial de cada achado contra o código real → síntese). 87 agentes, ~3.5M tokens.

**Baseline de saúde:** `npx tsc --noEmit` limpo · 62 suítes / 536 testes verdes · 0 testes skipados.

**Resultado:** 77 achados, **76 confirmados** (1 falso-positivo descartado). Severidade: **6 críticos · 34 altos · 29 médios · 7 baixos**.

---

## Veredito geral

O **motor de jogo é surpreendentemente rico e bem testado** para o estágio do projeto: simulação de partida por blocos com xG, momentum, fadiga, subs inteligentes, modificadores de formação/mentalidade/tempo/foco de ataque, cartões, pênaltis e faltas — tudo determinístico via RNG seedado e coberto por testes. Progressão tem curvas de idade/minutos/desempenho sensatas. Há board/confiança/objetivos, ~14 relatórios, transferências funcionais para o jogador humano.

Mas a auditoria revelou **um padrão sistêmico que atravessa quase todo o jogo:**

> **Muita lógica existe no `engine/` mas não está plugada no loop real do jogo, e o "mundo da IA" é cidadão de segunda classe.**

Três consequências estruturais dominam o relatório:

1. **O mundo da IA é inerte e decai.** Toda partida que não é do jogador é resolvida por um *coin-flip baseado só em reputação* (`simulateAiMatch`), ignorando o engine real. Clubes da IA **nunca pagam salários, nunca recebem receita, nunca geram base, nunca atualizam valor de mercado**. Táticas/elenco/contratações da IA não afetam nada. Ao longo de poucas temporadas a qualidade da liga **colapsa** (aposentadoria e transferências saem, nada entra).

2. **Sistemas inteiros existem mas estão desconectados.** Vantagem de casa, efeitos de comissão técnica (`getStaffEffects`), tela de foco de treino, recálculo de valor de mercado (`calculateMarketValue`), `advanceWeek` (finanças multi-clube) — **todos são código morto ou recebem inputs hardcoded**. O treino semanal passa `90/90 min, rating 6.5, foco balanced` fixos: minutos, desempenho e foco **nunca importam**.

3. **A camada de persistência/produto promete o que não entrega.** O menu lista **vários saves**, mas quase nenhuma tabela tem `save_id` — todos os saves compartilham e mutam o **mesmo mundo global**; criar um jogo novo **deleta dados dos outros saves**. Competições **nunca passam da 1ª fase** (campeão da copa é um jogo aleatório). Promoção/rebaixamento é **só cosmético** (nenhum clube troca de divisão). O **i18n cobre 7 de 41 telas** — o toggle de idioma quase não muda nada.

O que está **sólido**: o núcleo do match-engine (mecânica e testes), as queries SQLite (limpas e tipadas), a infra de i18n/tema (boa fundação, só subutilizada), a arquitetura geral de pastas.

## Os 6 críticos (deduplicados em 5 clusters)

| Cluster | O quê | Por que é crítico |
|---|---|---|
| **C1 · Save múltiplo é ilusão** | Tabelas de jogo (players, clubs, fixtures, finances, tactics, board, history) **não têm `save_id`** → mundo global compartilhado. `handleStartGame` roda `DELETE FROM club_finances`/`fixtures WHERE season=1`/`competition_entries` **sem escopo de save**. | Criar/jogar o save B **corrompe o save A**. O recurso de múltiplos saves exposto no menu é destrutivo. |
| **C2 · Copas/Champions param na 1ª fase** | `generateKnockoutRound` só é chamado 1×; nenhum código gera rodada ≥2 nem mata-mata da CL. `archiveKnockout` pega o "campeão" de um jogo aleatório da 1ª rodada. | Copas **nunca têm campeão real**; CL não tem fase final. Pilar do gênero quebrado. |
| **C3 · Promoção/rebaixamento não move clubes** | `season_relegated` é gravado, mas **nenhum `UPDATE clubs SET league_id`** existe. Promoção nem é registrada. | Os mesmos 20 times ficam em cada divisão **para sempre**. |
| **C4 · Treino semanal com inputs hardcoded** | `calculateWeeklyProgression(... 90, 90, 6.5, 'balanced')` literais → lógica de foco em `progression.ts:72-81` é **inalcançável**. | Desenvolvimento de jogador **não responde** a minutos, desempenho ou foco. A tela de treino é decorativa. |
| **C5 · Relatórios crasham ao tocar num jogador** | 10 chamadas `navigate('PlayerDetail', …)` mas a rota **não está registrada** no `RootNavigator`; `PlayerDetailScreen` é componente por props, não lê `route.params`. Sem ErrorBoundary. | Tocar em qualquer jogador nos relatórios Financeiro/Técnico/Base/Scout **derruba a tela** sem recuperação. |

## Altos, agrupados por tema (34)

- **Mundo da IA inerte:** IA simulada por reputação (ignora engine); IA não paga salários nem recebe receita; valor de mercado congelado no seed; elencos da IA erodem (sem base); IA só faz proposta pro elenco humano.
- **Lesões/cartões sem consequência:** lesão em jogo não seta `injury_weeks_left`; vermelho/amarelos não geram suspensão; jogador "lesionado" joga normal na semana seguinte.
- **Vantagem de casa morta:** `homeAdv` só escala `overall`, que a simulação nunca lê (empiricamente 38.6% casa × 39.1% fora; público 1k = 60k).
- **Progressão desconectada:** `getStaffEffects` código morto; `recalculatePotential` recebe `currentOverall=70` pra todos; tela de foco de treino só `useState`; veteranos não declinam.
- **Board/economia sem stakes:** ser demitido é só um texto (sem game-over); objetivos de copa/promoção **nunca podem ser cumpridos** (hardcoded `false`); orçamento vai a negativo sem falência/embargo; contrato expira mas jogador segue jogando e sendo pago; bug: free agent ainda no elenco e recebendo salário.
- **Persistência frágil:** sem índices nas FKs quentes (full-scan toda semana); rollover/novo-jogo sem transação (falha parcial = save corrompido); objetivos/trust/reputação colidem entre saves por `(club_id, season)`.
- **Telas órfãs:** 9 telas implementadas/stub **inacessíveis** (Squad, Treino, Base, Bracket de Copa, Artilheiros, Match Report); demissão sem game-over; `window.confirm` (web-only) crasha no nativo.
- **i18n veneer:** UI de transferências inteira em inglês; news-generator (~58 strings), assistant (41 templates) e objetivos do board hardcoded em inglês; relatórios hardcoded em **português** (toggle pra EN não muda nada).
- **Calendário colide:** liga/copa/CL caem na mesma semana e o loop só simula **1 jogo por clube por semana** — o outro some.
- **Tema:** helpers de cor copiados em 4 arquivos com hex fora da paleta (`#00e676`, `#ff9800`…).

### [CRITICAL] Weekly training feeds the engine hardcoded inputs; minutes, performance, focus never matter
- **Dimensão/Categoria/Esforço:** progression · bug · medium · _confirmed_
- **Arquivos:** `src/engine/game-loop.ts:483-492`, `src/engine/training/progression.ts:49-62`
- **Evidência:** calculateWeeklyProgression is called with minutesPlayedRecent 90, totalPossibleMinutes 90, avgRatingRecent 6.5, trainingFocus balanced, all literals, so every player trains as a full-time starter at flat 6.5 and the focus logic at 72-81 is unreachable
- **Impacto:** Playing time and form, the main development levers, are severed; stars and benchwarmers progress identically and managing minutes has zero effect
- **Correção sugerida:** Pass each player real recent minutes and avg rating from player_stats and the club selected training focus
- **Verificação:** Confirmed at game-loop.ts:487-490: the single call site of calculateWeeklyProgression passes hardcoded `minutesPlayedRecent: 90`, `totalPossibleMinutes: 90`, `avgRatingRecent: 6.5`, and `trainingFocus: 'balanced'` as literals. The DB already accumulates real per-player stats (avg_rating, minutes_played) in player_stats table — see database/queries/player-stats.ts:13-14, 27-28 and schema.ts:126-127 — but those values are never queried back for use in progression. The trainingFocus branching at progression.ts:72-81 (1.3x boost / 0.9x penalty) is unreachable dead code. The minutesFactor and performanceFactor functions at progression.ts:49-62 are correctly implemented but always receive 1.0 (90/90 = 100% minutes) and 1.0 (6.5 rating floor) respectively. Effort is medium: query recent player_stats before the progression loop, pass real values, and wire up a training-focus store/UI control.

The CL only generates group-stage round-robin fixtures (weeks 13-18) and never produces knockout/final fixtures despite format 'group_knockout'. Consequently in archiveKnockout (season-archiver.ts:303) maxRound is always 1, so the cup 'champion' is taken from a random first-round match (finals.sort by id, line 307), and the CL is archived as a knockout with no final at all.
- **Impacto:** Cup and continental competitions are non-functional: no bracket progresses, no real champion is ever crowned, the CL group stage leads nowhere. season_competition_results records a meaningless 'champion' (a team that won one early-round game). Across every season this pillar of a football manager is entirely absent, and the 'dynamically generated' comment is false.
- **Correção sugerida:** After each knockout round's fixtures are all played (detect in week-advance), gather winners, generate the next round via generateKnockoutRound, and persist it; repeat until one team remains. For the CL, take the top 2 of each group into a knockout bracket. Only then does archiveKnockout's maxRound logic identify a true final.
- **Verificação:** Fully confirmed. Evidence:

2. `src/engine/competition/calendar.ts:128-143` — CL generates group-stage round-robin only. No knockout phase (semis, final) is ever generated. Competition format is declared `group_knockout` but only the `group` half is implemented.

3. `src/engine/competition/fixture-generator.ts:70-83` — `generateKnockoutRound` works correctly but has exactly ONE call site in the entire codebase (`calendar.ts:99`, round 1 only). Grep of all `src/` confirms zero other call sites.

4. `src/engine/game-loop.ts` — `advanceGameWeek` simulates fixtures that exist for the current week; it never creates new fixtures. No post-match hook generates next-round fixtures.

5. `src/engine/history/season-archiver.ts:303-307` — `archiveKnockout` correctly picks from the highest `round` number among played fixtures. Since only round 1 exists, `maxRound` is always 1, so a first-round winner is recorded as "cup champion".

6. `src/screens/league/CupBracketScreen.tsx:5-16` — the entire screen is a non-functional stub: static text "Draw will be made when the cup begins", no bracket data rendered.

### [CRITICAL] Promotion/relegation is recorded but never physically moves clubs between divisions
- **Dimensão/Categoria/Esforço:** competition-season · missing-feature · large · _confirmed_
- **Arquivos:** `src/engine/history/season-archiver.ts:349-356`, `src/screens/EndOfSeasonScreen.tsx:325-530`, `scripts/data/leagues.ts:21-149`, `src/database/schema.ts:281`
- **Evidência:** The seed data defines a full pyramid (Premier League div 1 → Championship div 2 → League One div 3 ... per country, with promotionSpots/relegationSpots set, e.g. leagues.json shows id 1 div 1 releg 3, id 2 div 2 promo 3 releg 3). archiveLeague writes the bottom clubs to season_relegated (lines 349-356), but a full-tree grep for 'UPDATE clubs SET league_id' / any club league_id mutation returns NOTHING — no code ever moves a club to another league. EndOfSeasonScreen.handleContinue (lines 325-530) regenerates the calendar from the existing clubsByLeague and never swaps clubs between divisions. Promotion is never even recorded (no season_promoted table exists; schema.ts only has season_relegated at line 281), and EndOfSeasonScreen always passes wasPromoted:false (line 296).
- **Impacto:** Across all seasons the exact same 20 clubs stay in each division forever. Relegation is a cosmetic label with no gameplay consequence; promotion does not exist. The board trust/objective system reads wasRelegated from season_relegated but the player's squad and league never actually change division, breaking the core long-term progression loop of the genre.
- **Correção sugerida:** On season rollover, for each linked pair of divisions, swap the relegationSpots bottom clubs of the higher league with the promotionSpots top clubs of the lower league via UPDATE clubs SET league_id, BEFORE regenerating the calendar. Add a season_promoted table and wire wasPromoted from it into the board logic.
- **Verificação:** Fully confirmed. Every `UPDATE clubs` SQL in the codebase touches only `budget` or `reputation` — `league_id` is never mutated anywhere. Evidence: (1) `season-archiver.ts:349-356` inserts relegated clubs into `season_relegated` as a historical record only; no follow-up `UPDATE clubs SET league_id`. (2) A full grep of `src/` and `scripts/` for `UPDATE clubs SET league_id` returns nothing. (3) Schema at `schema.ts:281` has `season_relegated` but no `season_promoted` table. (4) `EndOfSeasonScreen.tsx:295` passes `wasPromoted: false` hardcoded. (5) `EndOfSeasonScreen.tsx:432-448` rebuilds the new-season calendar by calling `getClubsByLeague(dbHandle, league.id)` for each league unchanged — clubs never swap between leagues. (6) `leagues.ts` seeds `promotion_spots` into the DB but that field is never read by any engine or screen to drive actual movement. The board trust/reputation system (e.g. `processSeasonEndBoard`) does consume `wasRelegated` from `season_relegated`, so relegation has cosmetic trust consequences, but the squad and league membership never actually change. Implementing this properly requires: a post-archive step that reads `season_relegated` + computes promoted clubs (from next division up) and issues `UPDATE clubs SET league_id = ?` for each affected club; handling the player club-side (squads follow their clubs automatically via `club_id`); propagating the player-club's new league into the game store; and updating `handleContinue` to detect and set `wasPromoted` based on actual standing vs `promotion_spots`.

### [CRITICAL] Multi-save is exposed in UI but impossible: world tables have no save_id
- **Dimensão/Categoria/Esforço:** db-persistence · data-integrity · large · _confirmed_
- **Arquivos:** `src/database/schema.ts:68`, `src/database/schema.ts:50`, `src/database/schema.ts:168`, `src/database/schema.ts:141`, `src/database/schema.ts:320`, `src/screens/MainMenuScreen.tsx:103`, `src/screens/MainMenuScreen.tsx:55`
- **Evidência:** save_games is a real per-save row, but players, clubs, fixtures, competitions, club_finances, transfers, board_objectives, season_* history and tactics have NO save_id column (grep for save_id finds it only in assistants and board_trust). MainMenuScreen maps over getAllSaves() and loadSave() just sets store fields (game-store.ts:94) — every save reads/writes the SAME global player/club/fixture rows. Two saves managing different clubs share one mutable world; advancing one save ages players, runs transfers and edits budgets for the other save too.
- **Impacto:** The advertised 'Load Game' / multiple-saves feature is fundamentally non-functional. Running two saves cross-contaminates squads, finances, league tables and transfers. This silently destroys player progress and is a core data-integrity break.
- **Correção sugerida:** Either (a) constrain the product to a single save and remove the multi-save UI, or (b) add a save_id FK to every world table (players, clubs, fixtures, competitions, competition_entries, club_finances, transfers, transfer_offers, board_objectives, board_*_history, season_*, tactics, player_stats, match_events) plus composite indexes, and scope every query by save_id.
- **Verificação:** Fully confirmed by direct code inspection. Key evidence:

1. schema.ts:68-93 (players), :50-66 (clubs), :168-180 (fixtures), :141-149 (club_finances), :191-201 (transfers), :320-328 (board_objectives), :227-256 (tactics) — none have a save_id column. Only assistants (schema.ts:342) has save_id.

2. game-store.ts:94-109 — loadSave() only sets in-memory store fields; it does not scope DB reads to a save. All subsequent DB queries read the single shared world.

3. NewGameScreen.tsx:207-218 — the new-game flow executes a global DELETE: "DELETE FROM competition_entries; DELETE FROM competitions WHERE season = 1; DELETE FROM club_finances;" etc., destroying season-1 world data for ALL saves, not just the new one. This is the concrete cross-contamination path.

4. database-store.ts:62 — single shared SQLite file `football-manager.db` for all saves; no per-save database files.

5. saves.ts:81 — deleteSave() only removes the save_games row; world data (players, clubs, fixtures) is left orphaned/shared.

The advertised multi-save feature (MainMenuScreen.tsx:102-126 renders a list of saves to load) is architecturally broken. Every save reads and writes the same global player/club/fixture rows. Starting a second game corrupts the first save's calendar and finances immediately. Fixing this requires either (a) adding save_id FK columns to all world tables and scoping all queries — a major schema migration — or (b) switching to per-save database files.

### [HIGH] Home advantage and attendance scaling have zero effect on match outcomes
- **Dimensão/Categoria/Esforço:** sim-match · bug · small · _confirmed_
- **Arquivos:** `src/engine/simulation/team-strength.ts:111-113`, `src/engine/simulation/match-engine.ts:189-192`, `src/engine/simulation/match-engine.ts:325-332`, `src/engine/simulation/match-engine.ts:440-447`
- **Evidência:** homeAdvantageMultiplier() (match-engine.ts:189) scales home strength via calculateTeamStrength, but in team-strength.ts the multiplier is applied ONLY to `overall` (`if (isHome) overall *= homeAdv;`, line 111-113). The match engine's goal/shot probability uses team.strength.attack / opp.strength.defense / tempo / midfield (attackP at lines 440-447), never team.strength.overall — grep for `.overall` in match-engine.ts returns nothing. I ran 2000 equal-strength matches: home win 38.6% vs away win 39.1% (away actually wins more), and home goals are identical at 1.584 for attendance=1000 and attendance=60000. The whole #9 attendance-scaled home-advantage plumbing (HOME_ADVANTAGE_BASE/MAX, STADIUM_CAPACITY, attendance input) is inert.
- **Impacto:** A core football pillar is missing: playing at home gives no edge, and the attendance/stadium scaling that was clearly intended is non-functional. Results feel wrong, fixtures are effectively venue-agnostic, and stadium/attendance investment has no sporting payoff.
